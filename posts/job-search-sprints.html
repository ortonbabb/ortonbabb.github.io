Job Search Sprints

July 13, 2020 - 1 minute read -
Career

In Filtering Entry Level-Jobs using regex, I described a script that can filter jobs from the internet. In Calculating the Odds of Job Application Callbacks, I did some calculations that estimate that, given a 3% callback rate, about 200 of these job applications would be required to guarantee a job offer. In Career Design, I mentioned that both internet mining and networking would be required due to the existence of an unpublished job market. On the internet mining side, we can do a one month sprint to get to the 200 application goal.

Our goal is to submit 200 job applications over 20 working days (one month). The possibilities for the job search sprint are primarily constrained by the availability of jobs. Given the existing infrastructure mentioned above, finding the jobs that meet pre-established criteria and preparing the application is easy. The sprint requires an application rate of 10 jobs/day. Each day will have a job search time of three hours. This equates to 6 blocks of 30 minutes (20 minutes of work, with 10 minutes breaks). The first block is used to collect the jobs. Within each remaining block, two applications will be completed.
