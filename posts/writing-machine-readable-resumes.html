Writing Machine-Readable Résumés

July 16, 2020 - 3 minute read -
Career

Most job applications will give the option to upload a resume in any format. After parsing the upload, the applicant tracking system (ATS) automatically fills the various fields in the job form. Knowing this, we can structure our resumes to be machine-readable for optimal parsing.

For this post, I will use the example of the Workday Recruiting ATS, which can be recognized by URLs of the form company.myworkdayjobs.com. It’s the most common ATS used by large companies like Gartner, Capital One, Booz Allen Hamilton, etc.

What I did to design my resume was to look at the fields of the request and then go back and re-write my resume to match it. Here is what the fields for work experience looked like:

Instead of uploading a .docx or .pdf file, I used a simple .txt file with some markup conventions:

Resume.txt

Orton Babb
https://ortonbabb.com

(My Address Here)

Email: ortonbabb@gmail.com

Phone: (My Phone Number Here)

# Work Experience

Company: Mason Experimental Geometry Lab
Location: Fairfax, VA

To: December 2018

While I don’t know how the ATS parser works under the hood, I verified that this formatting works almost perfectly. All that’s left after uploading is to answer a few questions about whether you’ve worked at the company before, disability and veteran status etc.

The best part is that after this you don’t have to re-fill the application to apply to other jobs at the same company. Nice!
